PERCHANCE TO DREAM

Perchance to Dream is an exploration of Hamlet told through the music of Radiohead. It takes place in a world much like our own, where algorithms replace human connection and creative expression is commodified.

The story takes place at the Elsinore, a music venue and Chelsea Hotel sort of place, in a world that resembles, but is not, our own. It's set in the future, and explores our relationship to technology and consumerism, how it both isolates and brings us together.

Concert:

We held our premiere concert on 5/27/23 to a sold out, 400-person show at Pico Union Project in Los Angeles. It was covered by the LA Times, LAist (twice!), and CNN journalist David Daniel.

The concert was an abbreviated version of the full 2.5 hour show, which features 25 Radiohead songs along with the original Shakespeare text.

Creative Team

We are a collective of young artists who seek to push the boundaries of contemporary theatre, where the audience themselves are immersed in the story through the very technology that increasingly keeps us apart.

Dominique Star (concept, book, arrangement) is an actor, singer and musician with a background in music, theatre, and technology. She has performed with MIKA and along with acting, composes music for TV and Film.

David Su (musical direction, arrangement) is a recent graduate of the MIT Media Lab, where he studied under Tod Machover in the group Opera of the Future. He's particularly interested in how technology intersects with live performance, along with creating musical video games.

We're actively seeking to grow our team to help bring the full production to life.
